Delicious Sandesh

Ingredients

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1 tablespoon pistachios, finely chopped (for garnish)
  • A few strands of saffron (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring the milk to a boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  2. Once the milk is boiling, reduce the heat and add lemon juice gradually while stirring. The milk will curdle and separate into cheese and whey.
  3. Turn off the heat and let it rest for 5 minutes.
  4. Strain the curdled milk through a cheesecloth and rinse with cold water to remove the lemon flavor.
  5. Tie the cheesecloth and hang it for about 30 minutes to drain excess water. The resulting cheese is called chenna.
  6. Transfer the chenna to a clean, dry surface and knead it for 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and soft.
  7. Add powdered sugar and cardamom powder to the chenna and mix well.
  8. Divide the mixture into small portions and shape them into round or oval shapes.
  9. If using saffron, dissolve a few strands in 1 teaspoon of warm milk and add it to half of the mixture for variety in color.
  10. Garnish each Sandesh with finely chopped pistachios.
  11. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving.

Serving Suggestion

Serve Sandesh chilled as a dessert or sweet treat. It's perfect for festivals, celebrations, or as a delightful end to a meal. You can also experiment with different flavors by adding a little rose water, mango pulp, or cocoa powder to the mixture.

Pro Tips:

  • Use full-fat milk for the best texture and flavor.
  • Knead the chenna well to get a smooth texture in your Sandesh.
  • Adjust the sugar according to your taste preference.
  • Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days.
