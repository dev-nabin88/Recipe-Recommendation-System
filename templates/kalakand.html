Delicious Kalakand

Ingredients

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 2 tbsp chopped pistachios for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pan, bring the milk to a boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  2. Once the milk is boiling, add lemon juice gradually while stirring. The milk will curdle and separate into cheese and whey.
  3. Turn off the heat and let it rest for 5 minutes.
  4. Strain the curdled milk through a cheesecloth and rinse with cold water to remove the lemon flavor.
  5. Squeeze out excess water from the cheese (chenna) and transfer it back to the pan.
  6. Add sugar, milk powder, and cardamom powder to the chenna.
  7. Cook the mixture over medium heat, stirring continuously, until it thickens and starts leaving the sides of the pan (about 15-20 minutes).
  8. Add ghee and continue cooking for another 5 minutes until the mixture becomes grainy in texture.
  9. Grease a square baking dish with ghee and transfer the kalakand mixture into it.
  10. Spread the mixture evenly and smooth the top with a spatula.
  11. Sprinkle chopped pistachios on top and press them gently into the kalakand.
  12. Allow it to cool completely at room temperature (about 2-3 hours).
  13. Once set, cut into squares or diamond shapes.

Serving Suggestion

Serve Kalakand at room temperature or chilled as a dessert. It's perfect for festivals, celebrations, or as a sweet treat after meals. You can garnish with additional chopped pistachios or a sprinkle of cardamom powder just before serving for extra flavor and presentation.

Pro Tips:

  • Use full-fat milk for the best texture and flavor.
  • Stir continuously while cooking to prevent the mixture from sticking to the bottom of the pan.
  • The consistency of the mixture is key - it should be thick and slightly grainy before setting.
  • Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
