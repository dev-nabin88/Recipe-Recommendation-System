Delicious Mutton Kosha

Ingredients

  • 1 kg mutton, cut into medium-sized pieces
  • 3 large onions, finely sliced
  • 2 tbsp ginger paste
  • 2 tbsp garlic paste
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 4 tbsp mustard oil
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4 green cardamom pods
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 4 cloves
  • 2 tsp cumin powder
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Marinate the mutton pieces with yogurt, ginger paste, garlic paste, turmeric powder, and salt for at least 2 hours or overnight in the refrigerator.
  2. Heat mustard oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pan or kadai over medium heat. Add bay leaves, cardamom pods, cinnamon sticks, and cloves. Saut√© for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add sliced onions and cook until they turn golden brown, about 10-12 minutes.
  4. Add the marinated mutton to the pan and cook on high heat for 5-7 minutes, stirring frequently.
  5. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover the pan, and let the mutton cook in its own juices for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Add tomato puree, cumin powder, coriander powder, and red chili powder. Mix well and cook for another 5 minutes.
  7. Add about 1 cup of hot water, cover the pan, and simmer on low heat for 45-60 minutes or until the mutton is tender and the gravy has thickened.
  8. Once the mutton is cooked, add garam masala and ghee. Mix well and cook for another 5 minutes uncovered.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving.

Serving Suggestion

Serve hot Mutton Kosha with steamed rice, pulao, or Indian bread like naan or paratha. The rich, dark brown gravy and tender mutton pieces make this dish a perfect centerpiece for special occasions or weekend family meals. For a complete Bengali meal experience, serve it with a side of begun bhaja (fried eggplant) and a sweet dish like rasgulla or mishti doi.
