Refreshing Jal Jeera

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp cumin seeds (jeera)
  • 1 tbsp dried mint leaves
  • 1 tsp black salt (kala namak)
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper powder
  • 1/4 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 tsp amchur (dry mango powder)
  • 2 tbsp tamarind pulp
  • 2 tbsp jaggery or sugar
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 4 cups cold water
  • Ice cubes for serving
  • Boondi (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Dry roast cumin seeds in a pan until fragrant. Let them cool and then grind to a fine powder.
  2. In a blender, combine the roasted cumin powder, dried mint leaves, black salt, black pepper powder, asafoetida, amchur, tamarind pulp, jaggery (or sugar), and fresh mint leaves.
  3. Add about 1 cup of water and blend until smooth.
  4. Strain the mixture into a large jug or pitcher.
  5. Add the remaining water and lemon juice. Mix well.
  6. Taste and adjust the flavors if needed. Add more jaggery for sweetness or lemon juice for tanginess.
  7. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour to allow the flavors to meld.
  8. To serve, fill glasses with ice cubes and pour the Jal Jeera over them.
  9. Garnish with fresh mint leaves and a sprinkle of roasted cumin powder.
  10. If desired, add a tablespoon of boondi to each glass for extra crunch and flavor.

Serving Suggestion

Serve Jal Jeera chilled as a refreshing drink on hot summer days. It's perfect for quenching thirst and aiding digestion. You can also serve it as a welcome drink at parties or gatherings. For a fancy presentation, rim the glasses with a mixture of salt and roasted cumin powder.

Pro Tips:

  • For a stronger flavor, you can prepare the Jal Jeera concentrate and store it in the refrigerator. Dilute with water when serving.
  • Adjust the amount of jaggery or sugar based on your preference for sweetness.
  • You can add a splash of soda water for a fizzy version of Jal Jeera.
  • For a more intense flavor, let the mixture sit overnight in the refrigerator before serving.
