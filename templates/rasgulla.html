Delicious Rasgulla

Ingredients

For the Rasgulla:

  • 1 liter whole milk
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder

For the Sugar Syrup:

  • 2 cups sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 2-3 cardamom pods, crushed
  • 1 teaspoon rose water (optional)

Instructions

  1. Bring the milk to a boil in a large pot. Add lemon juice or vinegar and stir until the milk curdles.
  2. Strain the curdled milk through a cheesecloth and rinse with cold water to remove the sourness.
  3. Hang the cheesecloth with the chenna (curdled milk) for about 30 minutes to remove excess water.
  4. Transfer the chenna to a clean surface and knead it for 8-10 minutes until smooth.
  5. Add all-purpose flour and cardamom powder to the chenna and knead for another 5 minutes.
  6. Divide the mixture into small portions and roll into smooth balls.
  7. In a large pot, combine sugar and water for the syrup. Bring it to a boil.
  8. Add the crushed cardamom pods to the syrup and let it simmer.
  9. Gently add the chenna balls to the simmering syrup.
  10. Cover and cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes, or until the rasgullas double in size.
  11. Add rose water (if using) and let the rasgullas cool in the syrup.
  12. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving.

Serving Suggestion

Serve Rasgulla chilled as a dessert or a sweet treat. They can be garnished with chopped pistachios or a few strands of saffron for added flavor and presentation. Perfect for festivals, celebrations, or as a refreshing dessert after meals.

Pro Tips:

  • Make sure to knead the chenna well to get soft rasgullas.
  • Don't overcook the rasgullas as they may become hard.
  • The sugar syrup should be simmering, not boiling, when you add the chenna balls.
  • Store in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days.
