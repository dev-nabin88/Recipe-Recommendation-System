Creamy Malai Thandai

Ingredients

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1/4 cup almonds, soaked and peeled
  • 2 tbsp cashews, soaked
  • 2 tbsp pistachios, soaked
  • 1 tbsp poppy seeds (khus khus)
  • 1 tbsp fennel seeds (saunf)
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • A few strands of saffron
  • 2 tbsp rose water
  • 1/4 cup fresh cream (malai)
  • Ice cubes for serving

Instructions

  1. Soak almonds, cashews, and pistachios in water for 4-6 hours or overnight. Peel the almonds.
  2. In a blender, grind the soaked nuts, poppy seeds, fennel seeds, and black peppercorns into a fine paste using a little milk.
  3. In a large pot, bring the milk to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add the ground nut paste to the milk and mix well. Simmer for another 5 minutes.
  5. Add sugar and cardamom powder. Stir until the sugar dissolves completely.
  6. Remove from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
  7. Add saffron strands and rose water. Mix well.
  8. Refrigerate the mixture for at least 2 hours or until chilled.
  9. Before serving, whisk in the fresh cream (malai) until well combined.
  10. Pour the Malai Thandai into glasses filled with ice cubes.
  11. Garnish with chopped pistachios and a few saffron strands if desired.

Serving Suggestion

Serve Malai Thandai chilled as a refreshing drink during hot summer days or festive occasions like Holi and Mahashivratri. The creamy texture and rich flavors make it a perfect indulgence. For an extra special touch, you can rim the glasses with crushed pistachios or a mixture of sugar and cardamom powder.

Pro Tips:

  • For a smoother texture, strain the mixture before adding cream and chilling.
  • You can prepare the base in advance and add cream just before serving.
  • Adjust the consistency by adding more milk if the thandai is too thick.
  • For a vegan version, use almond milk and coconut cream instead of dairy.
