Delicious Gulab Jamun

Ingredients

For the Jamun:

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • Milk as needed (about 2-3 tbsp)
  • Oil or ghee for deep frying

For the Sugar Syrup:

  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 4-5 cardamom pods, crushed
  • 1 tsp rose water
  • Few strands of saffron (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking soda.
  2. Add ghee and mix well until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Gradually add milk and knead into a soft dough. Don't over-knead. Cover and let it rest for 10 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, prepare the sugar syrup by combining sugar and water in a pan.
  5. Bring the syrup to a boil, add cardamom pods, rose water, and saffron. Simmer until slightly sticky.
  6. Divide the dough into small portions and roll into smooth balls without any cracks.
  7. Heat oil or ghee for deep frying on medium-low heat.
  8. Fry the jamuns in batches, stirring gently until they turn golden brown.
  9. Remove from oil and immediately transfer to the warm sugar syrup.
  10. Let the jamuns soak in the syrup for at least 2 hours before serving.
  11. The jamuns will double in size after soaking in the syrup.

Serving Suggestion

Serve Gulab Jamun warm or at room temperature. They can be garnished with chopped pistachios or edible silver foil (vark). Perfect for festivals, celebrations, or as a decadent dessert after meals. For an extra special touch, serve with a small scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Pro Tips:

  • Make sure the oil temperature is medium-low while frying to ensure the jamuns cook evenly inside.
  • Don't over-knead the dough as it can make the jamuns hard.
  • The sugar syrup should be warm when adding the fried jamuns.
  • Store at room temperature for up to 2 days or refrigerate for up to a week.
