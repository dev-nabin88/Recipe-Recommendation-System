Refreshing Aam Panna

Ingredients

  • 2 large raw mangoes
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp roasted cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp black salt (kala namak)
  • 1/4 tsp regular salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper powder
  • A handful of fresh mint leaves
  • 4 cups cold water
  • Ice cubes for serving

Instructions

  1. Wash the raw mangoes and place them in a pressure cooker. Add about 1 cup of water.
  2. Cook the mangoes for 2-3 whistles or until they are soft and fully cooked.
  3. Once cooled, peel the mangoes and extract the pulp. Discard the seeds.
  4. In a blender, add the mango pulp, sugar, roasted cumin powder, black salt, regular salt, black pepper powder, and mint leaves.
  5. Blend everything until smooth and well combined.
  6. Transfer the blended mixture to a large jug or container.
  7. Add cold water and mix well. Taste and adjust sweetness or spices if needed.
  8. Refrigerate for at least an hour before serving.
  9. To serve, pour the Aam Panna into glasses filled with ice cubes.
  10. Garnish with fresh mint leaves and a sprinkle of roasted cumin powder if desired.

Serving Suggestion

Serve Aam Panna chilled on hot summer days. It's a perfect drink to beat the heat and stay hydrated. You can also add a splash of soda water for a fizzy version. For a party, serve in tall glasses with a slice of lemon and a sprig of mint for garnish.

Pro Tips:

  • Choose mangoes that are firm and slightly sour for the best flavor.
  • Adjust the sugar content based on the sourness of your mangoes.
  • You can store the concentrated Aam Panna mixture (without water) in the refrigerator for up to a week.
  • For a smoother texture, strain the blended mixture before adding water.
